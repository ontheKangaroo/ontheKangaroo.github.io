# MM Algorithm

Majorization-minimization algorithm (MM algorithm): replaces a difficult optimization prblem by a sequence of easier optimization problems. In most cases, the solutions of hte substitute problems converge to solution of the original problem.

The goal is to minimize the objective function L(θ):Rp→RL(\theta): \mathbb{R}^p \rightarrow \mathbb{R}L(θ):R​p​​→R.

  1. Create a surrogate function Q(θ∣θk):Rp×Rp→RQ(\theta | \theta^k): \mathbb{R}^p \times \mathbb{R}^p \rightarrow \mathbb{R}Q(θ∣θ​k​​):R​p​​×R​p​​→R satisfying

{Q(θk∣θk)=L(θk);Q(θ∣θk)≥L(θ),∀θ.\begin{cases} Q(\theta^k | \theta^k) = L(\theta^k);\\ Q(\theta | \theta^k) \geq L(\theta), \forall \theta. \end{cases} {​Q(θ​k​​∣θ​k​​)=L(θ​k​​);​Q(θ∣θ​k​​)≥L(θ),∀θ.​​

The function Q(θ∣θk)Q(\theta | \theta^k)Q(θ∣θ​k​​) is said to majorize L(θ)L(\theta)L(θ) at θk\theta^kθ​k​​.

  1. Choose θk+1\theta^{k + 1}θ​k+1​​ to minimize Q(θ∣θk)Q(\theta | \theta^k)Q(θ∣θ​k​​).
